About Me

Hi! My name is Anand Chaudhary. I was born in Nepal in the year 1990. I moved to the United States when I was 19 years of age, in the year 2010. I was still pursuing my bachelors degree when I got the opportunity to come to the US. I decided to complete my degree and I did so in the year 2012 and secured my Bachelors of Business Administration from the prestigious 178 years old college with rich curriculum, culture and diversity called the Madras Christian College.

One thing I really love about myself is my passion for learning new things. I think without this curiosity I would just be an empty shell of a person, just hollow on the inside. I have never stopped learning and will continue to do so till the end of my life. I love to play music, dance, sing, cook delicious food, write stuff, being on my laptop, outdoor sports, travelling, photography, creating arts and be in the midst of nature whenever time permits. I can also speak 6 different languages namely English, Nepali, Hindi and Urdu, Maithili, Tamil and understand Bengali to some degree. I can also play about 5 different musical instruments such as guitar, harmonica, trumpet, drums, a little bit of piano. Guitar off course is my favourite.

In my professional life I have worn several hats from being a school teacher to a sales person, a young businessman to a sales support specialist in an optical cabling manufacturing facility and even working as a Production Supervisor at Samsung. I felt the need to grow as an individual and do more challenging ventures in life and I found my new passion in my life as a Web Developer. I’m intrigued by the fact of how powerful my computer is and things I can build with it. Now, I have completely invested and dedicated my time in the world of Information Technology learning programming languages and technologies in the field of full stack web development. Every single day I’m exponentially growing as a developer learning new tools and technologies and integrating them into my projects. Initially I started coding in the early 2017 and started as a Business Analyst trainee and the more I learned about the application development and product requirements the more interested I got towards programming. For this reason, I joined the coding bootcamp at Northwestern University and can proudly say it was one of the best decisions I've made because I’ve learned so many new concepts, tools and technologies to build applications that I could not even imagine of.
